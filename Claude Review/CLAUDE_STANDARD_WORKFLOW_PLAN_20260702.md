## PART 1: Standard Resume Tailoring Workflow

### 1.1 How it works today

`build_resume()` runs roughly twenty sequential in-place mutations on the unpacked document XML: supported rewrites, outcome-framing rewrites, summary rewrite, role summary optimization, consulting/value/startup story rewrites (value and startup passes run twice), summary condensation, ERP rebalancing, low-fit bullet merge and removal, competency rename/remove/add, aggressive keyword-gap closure, bullet reorder, two-page selection, cutover limiting, non-ERP language scrubbing, formatting passes. Then about twelve hard assertions fire (summary length/structure, fonts, spacing, competency format, language rules), then `final_fit_audit`, `resume_readiness_report`, notes, ATS validation, and render check. Tailoring intelligence is keyword-driven: `keyword_set`/`audit_keywords` extract terms from the whole JD, `keyword_placement_audit` finds gaps, `alignment_score_report` scores keyword coverage (30), lane fit (25), business context (25), and outcome density (20) against a 115 cap with a fail floor of 86.

Genuinely good and worth protecting: source-truth snapshot validation, mandatory reorg sentence restoration, two-page fit enforcement, same-company overlap variants, ATS plain-text validation, FAIL/BRIDGE/POOR honesty in filenames, and the Resume Notes evidence map.

### 1.2 Findings, severity-ranked, with live evidence

**S1 - Term-substitution passes corrupt sentences and nothing catches it.** The live Foundant summary reads: "Led role-based training and enablement through the **enterprise platform to enterprise platform transition** for 150+ users." The non-ERP scrubber (`scrub_non_erp_resume_language`, `replace_cutover_terms`, `question_prep.scrub_cover_answer_for_job`) replaced both "Aptean Intuitive" and "Epicor Kinetic" with the same generic phrase inside one sentence, producing nonsense on a submitted document. The same defect family appears in the Tyfone interview guide (Part 2). Substitution is blind find-replace with no post-substitution grammar, article, or duplicate check. This is the single most damaging bug in the system because it manufactures obviously machine-generated text on otherwise strong outputs.

**S1 - Keyword extraction is a proxy for requirements, and its junk becomes visible resume content.** `audit_keywords` (resume_analysis.py ~1160) is a fifteen-clause boolean gauntlet over frequency-based token candidates. The live Foundant Resume Notes show its output: "Keyword placement gap: **adjust training** is missing from the resume" (a verb-object shard from an instruction sentence, not a requirement), and bridge blockers for "**multiple**" and "**impact**" (bare stopwords, already flagged in the companion document). Worse, `aggressively_close_supported_keyword_gaps` feeds keywords classified supported-direct straight into `add_targeted_core_competencies`, meaning extractor junk can be printed onto the resume as Core Competencies items. That is keyword stuffing generated by the system itself, the exact ATS-era behavior recruiters now screen against. There is no notion of a requirement element anywhere in the commercial path: the JD's actual responsibility and qualification statements (Foundant's "Key Objectives" list, for example) are never parsed as units, matched to evidence, or verified as covered.

**S2 - Twenty mutation passes with order coupling and no intermediate model.** Rewrite passes run on XML text, later passes re-edit earlier passes' output (`apply_value_story_rewrites` and `apply_startup_operator_rewrites` each run twice, scrubbing runs after all rewrites), and every pass re-parses the document. The enterprise-platform corruption above is exactly the class of bug this architecture breeds: pass N produces a sentence pass N+4 was never designed to see. There is no in-memory resume model that passes operate on and a single renderer serializes; the document XML is simultaneously the workspace and the output.

**S2 - Referee-without-repair, same as cover letters.** Twelve assertions can SystemExit after the entire assembly has run, discarding the work instead of repairing the specific violation. The companion document's repair-loop design (registry rule, mechanical fixer, re-validate, DRAFT fallback) applies here unchanged.

**S2 - Decorated titles pollute the header.** The Foundant header line prints "Associate Implementation Consultant | **Strategic Advancement Focus** | Enterprise SaaS | Enterprise Systems": the user-added JD suffix rendered as if it were a professional credential, plus near-duplicate segments ("Enterprise SaaS" / "Enterprise Systems") that `header_candidate_is_redundant` failed to collapse. The ingestion-point title normalization from the companion document fixes the first; the header builder needs a semantic-overlap dedupe for the second.

**S2 - The alignment score measures proxies, not coverage.** Keyword coverage, lane fit, business context, and outcome density are all indirect. A resume can score 98+ while a JD's named requirement goes unaddressed, and can score 86 (fail) because the extractor produced junk gaps like "adjust training." Once requirement elements exist (Part 3), the score's anchor should become requirement coverage: what fraction of parsed JD requirement elements are Direct-covered in the visible resume text, with keyword coverage demoted to a secondary signal.

**S3 - Summary register and mechanical polish.** The Foundant summary says "**Best in implementations needing** role-based enablement, workflow follow-through, user adoption, and quality control while keeping operating continuity **as visible as scope, quality, and timeline control**" - phrase-bank register, with "role-based enablement" appearing twice in three sentences. The companion document's grammar-safe assembler, slotted full-sentence banks, and structural writing checks cover this; the commercial summary and role-summary composers must migrate onto them, not just the federal ones. Also: bullet-ending punctuation is inconsistent within single roles (some bullets end with periods, some without), and the `.context` docs reference `config/language_rules.py` and `config/job_profiles.py` while the real path is `scripts/config/`, which misleads every future review thread.

### 1.3 Standard resume fix plan

1. **Substitution safety layer (highest priority, shared by every output).** Wrap all generic-term scrubbing behind one helper that, after substitution, checks the affected sentence for: the same replacement phrase appearing twice, "X to X" constructions, dropped articles ("around software platform"), and list items duplicated by substitution. On detection it repairs (use distinct natural phrasings such as "the legacy ERP" and "the successor platform," or restructure to "during the platform migration") or reverts and flags. Add a regression assertion that no output ever contains a repeated generic-platform phrase within one sentence or list.
2. **Requirement-element tailoring for commercial JDs.** Reuse the federal engine's Stage A parser (generalized to commercial headings: Responsibilities, What You'll Do, Key Objectives, Qualifications), Stage B term canon, Stage C evidence ontology, Stage D matcher, and Stage F coverage gate. The commercial mode differs only in output behavior: instead of hard-failing on uncovered Direct elements, it drives bullet selection, summary emphasis, and the Notes evidence map, and it feeds the fit audit. `FIT_PROFILES`, `TAILORING_EMPHASIS_PROFILES`, and lane logic stay as priors; elements become the ground truth.
3. **Keyword extractor v2.** Scope candidate extraction to requirement-bearing sections only (never About/Culture/Where You'll Work text, which is where "adjust training" and "impact" came from), extract noun phrases rather than frequency shards, and classify each candidate as skill/tool, domain, activity, or noise using the term canon. Only skill/tool and domain terms may ever reach Core Competencies injection, with a per-build cap and a human-legibility rule (a competency item must be a real thing a person can claim, not an adverb or verb shard). Readiness blockers may only come from requirement elements, never from raw keyword gaps.
4. **Model-then-render refactor (staged, behind regression cover).** Introduce an in-memory resume model (sections, roles, summaries, bullets as objects with provenance tags back to source-truth entries). Rewrite passes become model transforms; a single renderer serializes to XML once; formatting passes operate only on the rendered document. Do this incrementally: first move the summary and role-summary composers onto the model, then bullets, keeping snapshot validation as the safety net at each step.
5. **Repair loop before assertions**, using the shared validation registry from the companion document: each assertion gets a rule ID and, where mechanical, a fixer; unrepairable violations produce a DRAFT-suffixed output plus notes instead of a dead run.
6. **Header builder fixes:** consume the ingestion-normalized title; add semantic dedupe so no two header segments share a dominant word; never render user-added JD suffixes as header segments.
7. **Alignment score v2:** requirement coverage becomes the primary component; publish the per-element coverage table into Resume Notes exactly as the federal coverage matrix does, so every commercial build also leaves nothing to interpretation.
8. **Polish rules in shared config:** uniform bullet-ending punctuation normalizer; fix `.context` docs to the real `scripts/config/` paths.

## PART 2: Standard Interview Guides (Cheat Sheet and Detailed Guide)

### 2.1 What is already strong

The coaching architecture is genuinely good and should not be flattened: HNARBC and PREP frameworks with explicit rehearsal philosophy, timing ladders (15/30/60-90 seconds), filler-word and inflection drills, debrief-history integration that generated an accurate "Rambler risk" read for Tyfone, offer blockers, and interviewer trust questions. These sections read like a real coach. The defects are in the generated *answer text*, not the coaching structure.

### 2.2 Findings, severity-ranked, with live evidence

**S1 - The scrubber corrupts spoken answers worse than resumes.** The Tyfone guide's skills answer reads: "...Salesforce AppExchange, ServiceNow, Jira, **enterprise platform, software platform, successor platform, enterprise platform**, LivePerson LiveEngage, and Claude." Four generic placeholders, one duplicated, in a list Christian is supposed to say out loud. Another answer says "guiding onboarding, adoption, renewal risk, and expansion conversations **around software platform**" (dropped article, meaningless noun). Same root cause as Part 1 finding one; the substitution safety layer must run on every interview output too, and for spoken answers the correct repair is usually deletion of the redundant placeholder items rather than rewording.

**S1 - Resume-register text is pasted into SAY THIS boxes.** Two spoken answers in the Tyfone guide end with the mandatory resume sentence "**Position impacted by company reorganization.**" and carry resume-summary syntax ("Protected delivery quality and customer trust across 80+ international manufacturing clients in a $6M+ book of business by running full-lifecycle software platform delivery from discovery and configuration through data migration, testing, training, and post-go-live adoption, partnering with Product Management and Product Ownership on an Agile development team throughout"). Nobody speaks a 45-word single-sentence chain, and the reorg sentence is a resume compliance rule, not a spoken line. The guides need a spoken-register converter: when answer text is sourced from resume or qualification material, split to max ~18-word sentences, convert to first person naturally, strip resume-mandate sentences (the reorg fact belongs in the guide as its own honest talking point, phrased for speech: "The position was impacted by a company reorganization"), and re-run the structural writing checks in fail mode for these blocks.

**S2 - Question banks are not filtered by role type.** The Tyfone (fintech SaaS) guide includes the federal question "How many years of business-related experience do you have with public agencies or cooperatives?" with a rehearsed "0 years" answer, and the companion document found commercial SaaS/AI questions inside the IRS Federal Qualifications Statement. `question_prep` needs bank tags (federal, commercial, SaaS, consulting, generic) with builders selecting by detected JD type, so each output carries only questions that could actually be asked.

**S2 - Slot-filled template phrases repeat verbatim and collide with their own slots.** The phrase "technology-enabled change adoption, stakeholder alignment, and resistance risk in financial services operations" appears verbatim in multiple sections, and one answer opens "Executive **stakeholder alignment** has been a consistent part of the work, especially in environments where technology-enabled change adoption, **stakeholder alignment**, and resistance risk mattered" - the slot value duplicates a word already fixed in the template. Two rules for the shared assembler: a slot value may not repeat a content word adjacent to it in the template, and a filled phrase longer than six words may appear at most once per document (subsequent uses draw from registered paraphrase variants).
 
**S2 - Cross-answer duplication.** The same proof clause ("partnering with Product Management and Product Ownership on an Agile development team throughout," "$6M+ book of business") recurs across multiple SAY THIS answers in one guide. A rehearsing candidate then sounds scripted precisely where the guide's own coaching says to sound fresh. Add a per-document proof-clause ledger: each major proof fact gets used in full once, then referenced in shortened form ("the 80+ client portfolio I mentioned").

**S3 - Fact drift.** "McKinsey Forward **Learners**" appears where other outputs say "McKinsey Forward Program." Professional-development names must come from one source-truth constant, not per-script strings.

### 2.3 Interview guide fix plan

1. Run the substitution safety layer (1.3.1) on all interview outputs; for spoken lists, repair by deleting redundant placeholders.
2. Build the spoken-register converter in `question_prep.py` and route every SAY THIS/answer block through it; enforce with a spoken-answer check set (max sentence length, no resume-mandate sentences, first-person consistency) that fails the build for guides, since guides are rehearsal-critical even though they are prep outputs.
3. Tag question banks by role type and filter at build time; add a smoke assertion that federal-only questions never appear in commercial outputs and vice versa.
4. Add the slot-overlap and once-per-document phrase rules to the shared assembler; add the proof-clause ledger to guide composition.
5. Centralize professional-development and certification names into source-truth constants shared by all builders.
6. Requirement-element reuse: the parsed JD elements from Part 3 should also drive question prediction and story selection (an element with Adjacent coverage is a high-probability probing question and should get a prepared bridge answer), replacing part of the current keyword-trigger logic.

## PART 3: One Engine, Five Outputs

The companion document defined the federal requirement engine (announcement parsing, evidence ontology, matching, terminology injection, coverage gating). This document's findings confirm the same holes exist commercially. The end state is one shared core in `scripts/` plus `scripts/config/`:

1. **Requirement engine** (parse JD into elements, match to evidence ontology, classify Direct/Adjacent/Unsupported) feeding: federal resume (hard coverage gate), commercial resume (bullet selection, summary emphasis, alignment score, notes), cover letters (proof selection, bridge decisions), qualifications statements (per-element mapping), interview guides (question prediction, story selection).
2. **Validation registry with repair loops** (companion Part 2.3.3-4) governing resume assertions, cover letter QC, and spoken-answer checks, all with rule IDs, mechanical fixers, and DRAFT fallbacks.
3. **Substitution safety layer** (1.3.1) wrapping every generic-term scrub in every builder.
4. **Grammar-safe sentence assembler and slotted full-sentence banks** (companion Part 3.2) used by all prose composers, extended with the slot-overlap and once-per-document rules from 2.3.4.
5. **Evidence ontology** as the single machine-readable source of supported claims, scope facts, never-claim patterns, and canonical names (including certification/program names), consumed by everything.

## PART 4: Implementation Order and Regression

Extends the companion document's Part 4 sequence; steps 1-5 there (stopwords, title normalization, assembler, registry, cover repair loop) remain first. Then:

6. Substitution safety layer + corruption regression test: assert across all regenerated outputs that no sentence or list contains a repeated generic-platform phrase, an "X to X" construction, or a bare "software platform"/"enterprise platform" without an article. Validate against the live Foundant resume and Tyfone guide defects specifically.
7. Keyword extractor v2 with section scoping and candidate classification. Validate: Foundant notes contain no verb-shard or stopword gaps; competency injection proposes only skill/domain terms.
8. Evidence ontology, then the requirement engine, landing federal-first (companion steps 6-8), then switching the commercial alignment score and readiness blockers onto elements.
9. Spoken-register converter, question-bank tagging, slot-overlap and phrase-ledger rules. Validate: regenerate the Tyfone guide; zero placeholder corruption, zero resume-mandate sentences in answers, zero federal questions, no verbatim repeated filled phrase.
10. Model-then-render refactor of build_resume.py, staged (summary first, then role summaries, then bullets), each stage behind the golden-JD regression corpus, which now also renders one cheat sheet and one detailed guide per lane.
11. Header dedupe, punctuation normalizer, docs path corrections, name constants.

Each step ends with `scripts/smoke_test.py` plus the named validation, and steps 6-10 add their assertions to the regression harness so none of these defect classes can silently return.

## PART 5: Do Not Break

Source-truth snapshot validation, mandatory reorg sentences on the resume (removed only from spoken answers), two-page fit, Word-only outputs, FAIL/BRIDGE/POOR filename honesty, same-company overlap variation, ATS plain-text validation, debrief-history integration in guides, and the coaching frameworks (HNARBC, PREP, timing ladders, offer blockers, trust questions), which are the strongest human-sounding content in the system today.
