## Insights & Prep for Next Round

### What Landed Well

- Motivation tied to helping people in vulnerable moments — strong alignment with claims work
- Inventory adjustment example showed the full arc: problem → root cause → controls → automation → adoption
- Asking smart, role-focused questions (assignment, growth, challenges, advice) instead of leading with compensation
- Honest about not holding formal Lean Six Sigma certification — credibility move

### Risks / Gaps to Tighten

- Lean Six Sigma fluency: methodology is applied, not formal. Be ready to map your work to DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control) language
- Verbal delivery had some run-on answers — Christian self-flagged “not sure if I overtalked it.” Tighten stories to ~2 minutes using STAR or DMAIC structure
- Insurance / property and casualty claims domain knowledge is limited — worth reading up on auto injury, total loss, property complex, and weather vs. non-weather homeowners claims before the panel

### How to Prep for the Panel + Data Assignment

- Re-frame the inventory adjustment story explicitly in DMAIC terms with quantified outcomes (time saved, error reduction, adoption rate)
- Prepare 2–3 additional process improvement stories with measurable impact in case the panel asks for variety
- For the Excel + PowerPoint exercise:
  - Lead with the headline insight, not the methodology
  - Use 1 slide for key trends, 1 slide for root-cause analysis, 1 slide for recommended improvements
  - Tie findings back to claims employee experience and policyholder outcomes — mirror Katie’s framing
  - Practice the 10–15 minute presentation out loud; leave room for Q&A
- Prepare panel-specific questions: biggest current pain point in their segment, what a successful first project looks like, how process engineers partner with claims leadership
