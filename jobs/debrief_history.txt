### MTech Systems and Amino

- B2B SaaS, headquartered in Dunwoody, GA; operating since 1992; 300 employees globally, 65 countries
- 92% US market share in poultry production; major clients include Tyson, Purdue, Sanderson's
- Core business: software for live animal production, primarily poultry
- Amino (flagship, launched 2017): fully cloud-based, web platform
 - Business value: replaces fragmented per-segment systems with a single source of truth
 - Each stage of production (feed mills, grow-out, processing, distribution) historically ran separate systems
 - Amino consolidates all data and operations into one platform instead of managing five
 - Three core modules:
 - Supply chain: collects lifecycle data from egg to distribution (weight, feed consumption, mortality over ~24 weeks)
 - Financial and accounting: budgeting, forecasting, cost variance analysis, per-unit and per-flock cost tracking
 - Dashboards and reporting: turns operational and financial data into executive-level decision tools
 - All reporting built internally via SQL scripts; dedicated integration team owns custom report generation
 - Closest analogy is an ERP, though Jonathan noted Amino is more specialized and complex

### Project Consultant Role and Expectations

- Title is "Implementation Specialist" in industry standard; MTech calls it "Project Consultant"
- Implementation scale: 9 months to 2 years per project, team of 3-5 consultants per engagement
- Tier 1 ramp (first 3-4 months): support function across 2-3 active projects
 - Tasks: UAT testing, data validation, documentation
 - Goal: absorb the industry, the system, and the client culture before owning modules
 - Clients range from large enterprise (Tyson-scale) to individual farmers depending on module
- After ramp: own 2-3 modules independently (e.g., feed module, mobile)
 - Each module functions as a mini-implementation within the broader project
- Biggest challenge: industry and business knowledge, not technical skills
 - Poultry production has its own language, culture, and operational logic
 - Jonathan described it as "people speak a different language"
 - Technical complexity of Amino compounds the learning curve on top of industry knowledge

### Interview Process and Fit

 1. Prescreen with Jonathan Ho (this call)
 2. Hiring manager interview
 3. On-site, in-person final round
- Jonathan's feedback on fit:
 - ERP background is the closest analog to Amino; experience aligns well
 - Noted depth of experience across both sides of implementation (vendor and client)
 - Highlighted communication and relevant experience as positives
 - "I feel good about the resume, I feel better with your experience"
