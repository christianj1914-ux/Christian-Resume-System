About the Role

The AI Enablement Manager owns the delivery of Crowe’s firmwide AI Enablement strategy within our Studio business unit. This role leads the programs, learning, content, and measurement that drive adoption and effective, responsible use of AI technologies across Crowe’s business units, international affiliates, and select client engagements. The Manager is the connective tissue between Crowe’s internal customers, Studio resources, and external technology partners — setting the enablement roadmap, building the curriculum and community that scale AI fluency, and reporting adoption and impact directly to senior firm leadership. The role carries end-to-end ownership: it sets direction, executes, and is accountable for outcomes rather than supporting another owner.

What You Will Do

Own the Enablement Strategy & Roadmap: Set and drive Crowe’s firmwide AI Enablement strategy across internal business units, international affiliates, and select client programs — translating leadership priorities into a concrete, sequenced roadmap and being accountable for its execution.

Design & Deliver the Learning Curriculum: Own a multi-level AI training curriculum spanning foundational to advanced skills across the firm’s tool portfolio (e.g., ChatGPT Enterprise, Microsoft Copilot, Claude). Design, facilitate, and continuously refresh workshops, demos, intern programs, and community sessions.

Lead the AI Champion Community: Run the firmwide AI Guild / champion network — office hours, recurring sessions, and resource sharing — building the peer ecosystem that sustains adoption beyond live facilitation.

Own Enablement Content & Assets: Direct the creation and maintenance of enablement materials, guides, reference libraries, the AI Hub / “which tool when” resources, and a short-form video pipeline (scripting through production and hosting).

Own Adoption Analytics & Executive Reporting: Produce recurring adoption and usage reporting across the AI tool portfolio for senior firm leadership, and build the impact measurement frameworks (baselines, value taxonomy) that translate usage into demonstrable business value.

Lead AI Governance & Responsible Use: Maintain and evolve the firm’s AI governance framework — autonomy tiers, intake and evaluation, and responsible-use guidance — partnering with AI Governance, InfoSec, and IT on approvals and policy.

Evaluate Tools & Manage Vendor Relationships: Lead early testing of new AI tools and features, gather and synthesize user feedback, and manage relationships with technology partners (e.g., Microsoft) and emerging-tool vendors, feeding requirements back to providers.

Shape Enablement Platforms: Contribute to the design and content architecture of scalable enablement platforms (e.g., an AI fluency coaching platform) that extend reach beyond instructor-led delivery.

Manage Stakeholders & Advocate: Serve as the senior point of contact for AI enablement across business units and represent Crowe’s AI programs in internal and external forums as an advocate for responsible AI innovation and adoption.

Lead & Develop Talent: Serve as hiring manager and mentor for junior enablement staff and coordinate the work of contributors supporting enablement initiatives.
