Key Themes to Prepare
1. The Feedback Loop (Product)
Be ready to explain how you handle a client asking for a feature the software doesn't currently support. 
The Answer: Explain that you look for a workaround using existing configuration first. If a code change is truly needed, you quantify the business impact (e.g., "3 other clients need this") before escalating it to product management.
2. Change Management (Services)
In manufacturing, the biggest hurdle isn't the software; it's getting floor workers to trust AI scheduling over their old Excel sheets.
The Answer: Emphasize that you focus on user adoption. Share an example of how you won over skeptical operators by showing them a quick win that made their daily routine easier.
3. Scope Creep vs. Customer Satisfaction
VPs worry about deployment timelines slipping because a customer keeps changing the requirements. 
The Answer: Demonstrate that you can hold a firm but polite line. You handle scope creep by documenting the requests, mapping out the impact on the timeline, and presenting it as a "Phase 2" initiative. 
Questions to Ask the VP
Asking sharp questions flips the interview and shows executive presence. Try these:
"How do the Product and Customer Service teams currently collaborate to prioritize the roadmap when a major client makes a feature request?"
"What is the biggest bottleneck your team currently faces during the onboarding and implementation phase?"
"How does your team define a 'successful deployment' beyond just hitting the go-live date?"
